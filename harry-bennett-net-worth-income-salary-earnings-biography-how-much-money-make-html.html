Template: a number of problems For much of the 1930s and 1940s, Harry Bennett (1892–1979) worked as an executive at the Ford Motor Company, where he was a former boxer and former Navy sailor. His most well-known position was as the chief of Ford’s Service Department, also known as Internal Security. Although he was employed by Ford, his anti-union methods, such as those used in The Battle of the Overpass, caused him to become a bitter opponent of the United Auto Workers. He was dismissed by Henry Ford II in 1945, and he died in 1979 as a result of natural causes. Bennett had many homes in Michigan, including Bennett’s Lodge in Farwell, a log cabin-style house in East Tawas, and Bennett’s Castle, which was situated on the Huron River in Ypsilanti. He died in Ypsilanti.
