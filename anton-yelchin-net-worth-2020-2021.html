Early Life

Anton Yelchin was born on March 11, 1989, in Saint Petersburg, Russia. He was born to Irina Korina and Viktor Yelchin, former figure skaters. His family is Jewish. According to Anton, his grandparents were victims of Stalin’s hard rules. He also mentioned that his parents qualified to participate in the 1972 Winter Olympics, but they were not allowed to go. They were prevented by the government authorities.

His family relocated to the United States in September 1989 as refugees. Anton was only six months at that time. His mother worked as a figure skating choreographer, and his father worked as a figure skating coach. He attended the Sherman Oaks Center for Enriched Studies in California. He also studied film at the University of Southern California. He loved playing the guitar.

His mother revealed that a relative saw Anton when he was still a little boy and said that he is a beautiful boy and that he would be an actor. His mother also mentioned that even though they tried to help him develop a passion for figure skating, he was not good at it.
