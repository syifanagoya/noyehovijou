To make the search much easier for you, we have put together a list of modern Xhosa names and their meanings. The names below were carefully picked and will be just perfect for your child.

Naming A Child In The Xhosa Culture – Background Details

The Xhosa language is considered South Africa’s second-most known used language after Zulu with an estimate of 8 million Xhosa people living in the country. Although about 200,000 Xhosa people can be found in Zimbabwe, South Africa has the highest number of Xhosa people. Just like Zulu, Xhosa people do not just give their children names as a mere means of identification. All Xhosa names usually have strong meanings and significance attached to them.

Some parents give their children names depending on the situation surrounding their births. Others may name a child depending on their feelings and beliefs. Also, children are named based on what the parents expect the child to become. In the Xhosa culture, names are usually considered more important than surnames and are used to trace ancestral history. When a woman gets married, she is expected to add her clan name to her new surname.

Based on the name numerology, these names have attributes attached to them, but that does not necessarily mean that your child will automatically have any of the attributes that come with his or her name.
