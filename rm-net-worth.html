His wealth primarily stems from his role as the leader of BTS, the global sensation that has taken the world by storm.

His solo career also plays a vital role. His mixtapes, RM and Mono, along with his studio album Indigo, have been well-received, contributing to his financial success.

RM is not just a musical genius; he’s also a savvy investor. He owns real estate in Seoul, including a $5.7 million flat in the prestigious Nine One Hannam complex.

FAQs about RM

What is RM’s real name?

RM’s real name is Kim Nam-Joon. He was born on September 12, 1994, in Yeouido-dong, Seoul, South Korea.

Why did RM change his stage name?

Originally, RM went by the name Rap Monster, which he felt no longer represented his musical identity. In November 2017, he changed it to RM, which he says can stand for Real Me.

How did he learn English?

He learned English by watching the American TV show Friends. He has mentioned that he watched the series multiple times with Korean subtitles, English subtitles, and eventually without subtitles.

What is his educational background?

He graduated from Global Cyber University with a degree in Broadcasting and Entertainment. He is also enrolled in the Master of Business Administration program in Advertising and Media at Hanyang Cyber University.

Does he have any nicknames?

Yes, he is affectionately called God of Destruction by fans and his fellow BTS members due to his knack for accidentally breaking things. Another nickname is Juni, used by his close friends and family.

What kind of music has he released as a solo artist?

He has released several solo projects, including his mixtapes RM and Mono. His latest full-length album is Indigo, which showcases his personal musical style and artistic growth.
